## Marvel at the Living Fort of Jaisalmer
Among the top **Things to do in Jaisalmer**, you cannot miss the legendary Jaisalmer Fort, famously called Sonar Quila. In contrast to most forts, this is a "living fort" which is home to around ten thousand residents. Roaming its cramped lanes, seeing the age-old Jain Temples, and seeing the sunset from the bastions are some of the must-have experiences. **Tunes of Dunes** suggests a guided heritage walk to be able to see and understand the detailed architecture and the lively local life that is going on in the fort's golden walls.

## Discover History and Hauntings
Jaisalmer's allure does not stop at the fort and **sand dunes**. Wandering around the Patwon Ki Haveli cluster is definitely one of the deeply cultural **Things to do in Jaisalmer**. The exquisite stone carvings and the gold-embellished ceilings speak volumes of the city's prosperous merchant past.

If you are gutsy, then you definitely have to see the **Kuldhara abandoned village**. According to the stories, the village was cursed and left empty in the dead of the night, and now it is full of ghostly remains, which happen to be quite interesting if you choose to explore with the **Tunes of Dunes' knowledgeable guides** answering your every question.

## Peaceful Moments at Gadisar Lake
To offset the adventure, it is worth mentioning that Jaisalmer has some of the most peaceful **Things to do in Jaisalmer** like a visit to Gadisar Lake. The aesthetically pleasing lake is an artificial water reservoir, which is surrounded by temples and shrines and gets its charm in the cold season. A relaxed boat trip would be an option. Otherwise, you can just sit at the steps of the riverbank and see numbers of nature's shows of the sunset reflecting the water. It is an ideal place for shooting and introspection before you immerse yourself once again in the lively city's markets to buy traditional textiles and camel bone jewelry.
